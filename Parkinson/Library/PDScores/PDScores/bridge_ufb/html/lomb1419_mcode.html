1function [f,P,prob] = lomb(t,h,ofac,hifac)
 2% LOMB(T,H,OFAC,HIFAC) computes the Lomb normalized periodogram (spectral
 3% power as a function of frequency) of a sequence of N data points H,
 4% sampled at times T, which are not necessarily evenly spaced. T and H must
 5% be vectors of equal size. The routine will calculate the spectral power
 6% for an increasing sequence of frequencies (in reciprocal units of the
 7% time array T) up to HIFAC times the average Nyquist frequency, with an
 8% oversampling factor of OFAC (typically >= 4).
 9% 
10% The returned values are arrays of frequencies considered (f), the
11% associated spectral power (P) and estimated significance of the power
12% values (prob).  Note: the significance returned is the false alarm
13% probability of the null hypothesis, i.e. that the data is composed of
14% independent gaussian random variables.  Low probability values indicate a
15% high degree of significance in the associated periodic signal.
16% 
17% Although this implementation is based on that described in Press,
18% Teukolsky, et al. Numerical Recipes  In C, section 13.8, rather than using
19% trigonometric rercurrences, this takes advantage of MATALB's array
20% operators to calculate the exact spectral power as defined in equation
21% 13.8.4 on page 577.  This may cause memory issues for large data sets and
22% frequency ranges.
23% 
24% Example    
25%    [f,P,prob] = lomb(t,h,4,1);   
26%    plot(f,P)
27%    [Pmax,jmax] = max(P)
28%    disp(['Most significant period is ',num2str(1/f(jmax)),...
29%         ' with FAP of ',num2str(prob(jmax))])
30% 
31% Written by Dmitry Savransky 21 May 2008
32
33%sample length and time span
34N = length(h);
35T = max(t) - min(t);
36
37%mean and variance 
38mu = mean(h);
39s2 = var(h);
40
41%calculate sampling frequencies
42f = (1/(T*ofac):1/(T*ofac):hifac*N/(2*T)).';
43
44%angular frequencies and constant offsets
45w = 2*pi*f;
46tau = atan2(sum(sin(2*w*t.'),2),sum(cos(2*w*t.'),2))./(2*w);
47
48%spectral power
49cterm = cos(w*t.' - repmat(w.*tau,1,length(t)));
50sterm = sin(w*t.' - repmat(w.*tau,1,length(t)));
51P = (sum(cterm*diag(h-mu),2).^2./sum(cterm.^2,2) + ...
52     sum(sterm*diag(h-mu),2).^2./sum(sterm.^2,2))/(2*s2);
53
54%estimate of the number of independent frequencies
55M=2*length(f)/ofac;
56
57%statistical significane of power
58prob = M*exp(-P);
59inds = prob > 0.01;
60prob(inds) = 1-(1-exp(-P(inds))).^M;
